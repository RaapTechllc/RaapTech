## 1. Procore
**URL:** https://www.procore.com  
**What they sell:** End-to-end construction management software for general contractors, owners, and specialty contractors.

**Hero Headline:** "Together, we can build it all"

**CTA Text:** "Get started today" / "Request a demo"

**Trust/Proof Elements:**
- "3 million+ global projects" statistic prominently displayed
- App store ratings shown inline (4.5/2,648 reviews, 4.6/4,074, 4.7/42K, 4.1/3,090)
- Segmented by user type (General Contractors, Owners, Specialty Contractors)
- References "2025 Dodge & Data Analytics ROI report"

**What makes it effective:** Procore leads with unity and partnership ("Together") rather than features. The social proof is massive and specific—3 million projects is a category-dominating number. Multiple review scores from different platforms creates a "wall of trust."

**RaapTech should steal:** Lead with a unifying mission statement, not a feature list. Display aggregate customer metrics prominently (total projects, total users, total value managed). Show multiple review scores side-by-side to create overwhelming social proof.

## Summary: Top Patterns Across Best Examples

### 1. Outcome-First, Feature-Second
The best homepages lead with what customers achieve, not what the product does:
- "Together, we can build it all" (Procore)
- "The Last Agency You'll Ever Hire" (Single Grain)
- "Run the job. Build with confidence" (Buildertrend)

**Pattern:** Hero = ultimate outcome or transformation. Features come below the fold.

### 2. Specific, Quantified Social Proof
Vague claims are replaced with hard numbers:
- "3 million+ global projects" (Procore)
- "$1B+ Rev Influenced" (Single Grain)
- "shave more than 5% off its losses" (Riskonnect/Wendy's quote)
- "20,000 builders" (Buildertrend)

**Pattern:** Use at least 3 specific metrics on the homepage. Round numbers for scale, precise numbers for outcomes.

### 3. Problem-Aware Framing
Top performers acknowledge pain before presenting solution:
- Buildertrend's industry statistics (22% still use spreadsheets, 27.5% delivered late)
- Single Grain's "If This Sounds Familiar" section
- BuildOps' "No chaos. No compromises." positioning

**Pattern:** Show you understand the problem deeply before claiming you solve it.

### 4. Consultation/Demo CTAs (Not "Sign Up")
High-ticket services use low-friction, high-touch CTAs:
- "Get a personalized demo" (Procore, Buildertrend)
- "Get Personal Demo" / "Join Weekly Demo" (BuildOps)
- "Senior strategist responds within 24 hours" (Single Grain)
- "See how GCPay can help your accounting team" (GCPay)

**Pattern:** Avoid "Start free trial" for $10K+ deals. Use "demo," "consultation," or "assessment" framing.

### 5. Industry-Specific Language
Winners speak their buyer's language:
- "Steel-toed smarts" (BuildOps)
- "Dispatch to final invoice" (BuildOps)
- "Pay apps," "lien waivers" (GCPay)
- "Jobsite," "foreman," "RFIs" (Fieldwire)

**Pattern:** Use 3-5 industry-specific terms in your hero and subhead to signal insider status.

## Recommended Homepage Structure for RaapTech

Based on patterns above, here's the recommended structure:

```
1. HERO SECTION
   - Outcome-focused headline (not feature-focused)
   - Subhead with specific metric or scope
   - Primary CTA: "Book a consultation" or "Get assessment"
   - Secondary CTA: "See case studies" or "View results"

2. SOCIAL PROOF BAR
   - 3-4 quantified metrics (clients served, value delivered, outcomes achieved)
   - Logo strip of recognizable customers

3. PROBLEM AWARENESS
   - "If you're experiencing X, Y, Z..." section
   - Industry pain point statistics if available
   - Self-qualification framing

4. METHODOLOGY/Framework
   - 3-4 step or pillar approach (memorable structure)
   - Visual representation of process
   - Emphasis on adoption/long-term success, not just delivery

5. OUTCOME SHOWCASE
   - 3 customer stories with specific, quantified results
   - Named customers with titles when possible
   - Mix of industries/use cases

6. TEAM CREDIBILITY
   - Founder/team credentials
   - Years of experience, notable past work
   - "Why we built this" narrative

7. FINAL CTA
   - Consultation/demo booking
   - Scarcity if real (limited slots, waitlist)
   - Low-friction alternative (newsletter, diagnostic, assessment)
```

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Leading with features instead of outcomes** — "We do X, Y, Z services" vs. "You'll achieve [result]"

2. **Vague social proof** — "Trusted by companies worldwide" vs. "3 million+ projects across 90 countries"

3. **Generic CTAs** — "Contact us" vs. "Get your personalized assessment" or "Book a strategy session"

4. **No problem acknowledgment** — Jumping straight to solution without showing you understand the pain

5. **Testimonials without specifics** — "Great to work with!" vs. "Reduced our claim volume by 34% in one year"

6. **Missing methodology** — No framework or process shown, making you seem like a commodity

7. **Ignoring buying committees** — Speaking to only one role when 4-6 stakeholders are involved

8. **No industry language** — Using generic business terms instead of trade-specific vocabulary

9. **Hiding team credentials** — Not showcasing who's actually doing the work and why they're qualified

10. **Free trial framing for high-ticket** — "Start free trial" sends wrong signal for $10K+ engagements
