## 0. Rules that apply to every page

**Positioning rules**
- Front-door language is **database + workflow outcomes only**. No "AI," no "automation-first," no "innovation" on homepage, hero, primary CTAs, or card headlines. These words may appear deeper (About, FAQ, interior pages) but never at the top of the funnel.
- Operator credibility leads. CAMduct / ESTmep / CADmep / Revit / Navisworks specificity leads. Knowledge retention leads. Everything else is support.
- Positioning anchor line (reuse selectively): *"Your database should be your edge. Not your bottleneck."*

**Proof discipline**
- Only claim what is true today. No implied Skool traction, no named case studies, no percentage lifts presented as fact without a cited client.
- Every representative metric must be **labeled explicitly** ("representative of typical Sprint engagements") and noted as replaceable once real client case studies close. See §5 Proof Language for the full safe-now / defer list.

**Sprint-first weighting**
- Sprint carries the homepage. Skool is the secondary path, visually smaller, framed as continuity *beside* the Sprint — not underneath it, not competing with it.
- Every page except `/skool` uses **Book Free Audit** as the primary CTA. Only `/skool` shifts the primary CTA to **Join Premium on Skool**.

## 3. `/sprint` page wire — section by section

### S1. Hero

- **H1:** The Fabrication Workflow Fix Sprint
- **Subhead:** Two weeks. One workflow. $4,800 fixed. Built for mechanical contractors running CAMduct and ESTmep who already know where the bottleneck is — and want it fixed by someone who has sat in your chair.
- **Primary CTA:** Book the Free Audit Call → `/contact#book`

### S2. The Promise

- One broken workflow in your shop — picked during the audit — fully rebuilt in 14 days.
- No new software. We rebuild what you already own so it works the way the shop floor actually needs it to.
- You keep every artifact. Nothing lives on our servers after we leave.

### S3. Who It's For

- Mechanical / sheet metal / HVAC / plumbing contractors, **20–200 employees**
- Already running **CAMduct and/or ESTmep**
- Have **one specific workflow** that hurts (RFQ, estimating → shop, or library)
- Can put **one decision-maker + one workflow owner** on calls over the two weeks

### S4. The Three Tracks (Pick One at the Audit Call)

- **Track A — RFQ → Qualified Quote.** Intake-to-first-response. Email parsing, qualification rules, templated acknowledgments, routing. *Target outcome:* first qualified response in under 15 minutes during business hours.
- **Track B — Estimating → Shop Handoff.** ESTmep / CAMduct / shop-floor pipeline. Takeoff data integrity, spool sheets, cut-list export, tablet or paper artifact. *Target outcome:* one source of truth from bid to cut, zero re-entry.
- **Track C — Library & Template Cleanup.** ITM libraries, MAJ files, connection and seam libraries, service templates, spec database. *Target outcome:* new estimator productive in week one, not week six.

### S5. Timeline

- **Week 1 — Audit & Rebuild Spec.** Forensic walk-through of actual files, templates, ITMs, MAJs, handoff points. One to three interviews. Written diagnosis of what's broken, why, and exactly what we're going to change.
- **Week 2 — Build, Document, Train.** Rebuild the workflow end-to-end. Library cleanup, template standardization, or integration work as the fix requires. Short searchable documentation. One live training session with the owners.
- **After handoff — 30-day support.** Slack, email, or phone. Fast response. Included.

### S6. Deliverables

- The rebuilt workflow, live in your environment
- Written runbook (screenshots, steps, decisions, fallbacks)
- Updated library files / templates / automation artifacts (you own them)
- One-page change log for your shop manager
- Recording of the training session
- 30-day support window

### S7. Guarantee

> If, at the end of the 30-day support window, the workflow we rebuilt is not measurably better than the one we replaced — on the metric we agreed on at the audit — we refund the Sprint fee in full. No arguments. No partial credits. We either fixed it or we didn't.

### S8. Fit Requirements (honesty filter)

Before the audit, you should answer *yes* to all five:
- We use CAMduct and/or ESTmep today
- 20 to 200 employees
- One specific workflow is costing us measurable time, dollars, or rework
- One decision-maker + one workflow owner can join calls over two weeks
- We want to own the fix ourselves after it's built

Say no to any of these and the Sprint isn't the right engagement. We'll tell you on the audit call.

### S9. FAQ

- **Does this include AI?** Sometimes. Automation — AI included — is a tool we use where it makes the workflow faster or more accurate. If your bottleneck needs a spreadsheet and a convention change, that's what you get. We pick the fix that works, not the fix that sounds impressive.
- **Do you need access to our systems?** Yes. Read access to CAMduct/ESTmep, the file shares where libraries live, and the handoff artifact. NDA on request.
- **Remote or on-site?** Either. Chicago metro includes one on-site day. Outside the metro, travel at cost — most Sprints are fully remote.
- **What if we want to continue after?** Two paths: another Sprint on a different workflow, or continuity inside RaapTech Skool. We don't push either.
- **Can you sign an NDA?** Yes.

### S10. Final CTA

- **H2:** Book the Free Audit.
- **Body:** 30 minutes. One call. No forms. If the Sprint is the right fix, we schedule it. If it isn't, you walk away with a useful diagnosis and no invoice.
- **Primary button:** Book Your Free Audit → `/contact#book`

## 5. Proof language — safe now vs defer

### Safe to use today (verifiable)

- "20+ years in mechanical contracting"
- "Local Union 73 certified, 5-year apprenticeship, TAB certified"
- "Autodesk Fabrication Expert: CADmep · CAMduct · ESTmep · Revit · Navisworks"
- "Apprentice to Operations Manager" (real career progression)
- "4 years and $50K+ personally testing construction and fabrication software"
- "We run 2 Sprints at a time" (capacity signal)
- "Currently accepting 2 new Sprint engagements for Q3 2026" (real schedule)
- "Representative of typical Sprint engagements" (when labeling illustrative metrics)

### Defer until real (do NOT ship prematurely)

- Named client case studies (name, logo, photo, attributed quote) — ship only after written client sign-off
- "Most Sprint clients continue into Skool Premium" — needs 3+ Sprints delivered AND 3+ graduated to Premium
- "Our members report…" / "Premium members have…" — needs real members with real outcomes
- "Join 50+ fabrication shops" or any member count — ship only once the count is not embarrassing
- Specific percentage lifts presented as fact without a cited client
- Unattributed testimonial quotes

### Bridge language (safe stand-ins until real proof exists)

- "Representative of typical Sprint engagements."
- "We replace these with named client case studies as engagements close."
- "Built for ongoing support after the Sprint."
- "Designed as the continuity layer beside the Sprint."
- "Premium is where the real problem-solving happens." (describes design intent, not traction)
